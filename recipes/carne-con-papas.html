Carne con papas

Description

Carne con Papas" is a traditional Latin American dish that translates to "Beef with Potatoes" in English. The dish typically features beef, often in the form of stewed or braised meat, cooked with potatoes and a variety of seasonings.

Preparation can vary across regions, and additional ingredients such as onions, garlic, bell peppers, tomatoes, and spices may be used to enhance flavor. The beef is usually cut into bite-sized pieces and cooked until tender, creating a hearty and flavorful stew. Potatoes are a common addition, contributing both substance and texture to the dish.

Carne con Papas is known for its comforting qualities and is often served with rice, bread, or other starches to complement the savory flavors of the beef and potatoes. The dish is popular in many Latin American countries, with each region putting its own spin on the recipe. It reflects the rich culinary traditions of the area, offering a satisfying and nourishing meal enjoyed by many.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup olive
  • 1 large white onion,cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 chopped green pepper
  • 1 cup chopped canned tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon dry oregano
  • 1 tablespoon dry cilantro
  • 2 1/2 pounds beef top round, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 cup dry Sherry
  • 4 large peeled potatoes, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • Salt and white pepper
  • White rice, as an accompaniment

Preparation

  1. In a heavy skillet, heat oil and saute onions for 5 minutes.
  2. Add garlic,peppers, tomatoes, spices, and meat and saute for 5 more minutes.
  3. Add Sherry and simmer, uncovered, 5 minutes.
  4. Add potatoes and enough water to cover meat and potatoes. Let simmer until the potatoes are tender, about 25 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper. Can be served with white rice.
